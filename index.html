About

The project SIDRO aims at representing, through an ontology, the debates arising around the possible interpretations of symbolism in artworks.
The case study for the application of our work is the painting "Guernica" by Picasso, because of the presence of numerous symbols, objects of many studies, and also for its historic and politic implications, which have fostered the debates.

Modelling

Theoretical model

We posed ourselves questions in order to describe an artwork from several points of view, from traditional metadata to interpretative issues.

Conceptual model

Starting from the questions defined in the theoretical model, we answered them using classes and predicates from already existing ontologies.

Our ontology: SIDRO

Since it was problematic to answer to those questions related to our focus, we developed a new ontology that satisfies this need, using Proteg√©.

Case study application

Our sources

We selected a sample of interesting essays and books about the painting and the interpretation of its symbols.

Automatic Knowledge Extraction

Using Python, we applied computational tools on our sources to automatically extract interesting features in order to accomplish our aim.

SIDRO on Guernica

We applied SIDRO's classes and predicates on the painting, in order to formally describe it.

SPARQL queries

After the formal description of our case study Guernica, using SPARQL queries in the platform Jena, we queried our information to discover if the obtained results were conformant to the inserted information.

Knowledge graph

In the end, we gathered all the knowledge extracted from the painting and, thanks to RDFGrapher, we created a graph visualisation.
